About MetaGuard

Three Frontier Engines

MetaGuard combines three cutting-edge meta-analysis methods that have no existing browser-based implementation:

1. Robust Pooling (Density Power Divergence)

Adjusts the influence of multiple outliers simultaneously using generalized likelihoods based on the density power divergence (DPD). Unlike conventional methods that assume at most one outlier, DPD-based estimation automatically downweights studies that deviate from the majority, providing robust point estimates, confidence intervals, and heterogeneity estimates even when multiple influential outlying studies are present.

Noma H, Nagashima K, Furukawa TA. Statistics in Medicine 2024;43:4006-4024.

2. P-Hacking Detection (RTMA + MAN)

Right-Truncated Meta-Analysis (RTMA) corrects for within-study p-hacking by imputing the entire underlying distribution of population effects. It models the truncation of non-affirmative results to estimate the true effect size distribution. Meta-Analysis of Non-affirmative studies (MAN) provides a conservative bound by pooling only studies whose results do not support the favoured hypothesis, as these are least susceptible to p-hacking.

Mathur MB. Research Synthesis Methods 2024;15:461-479.

3. MetaForest (ML Heterogeneity Explanation)

Adapts random forests to meta-analysis using precision-weighted bootstrap sampling. Each tree is grown on a weighted subsample where more precise studies exert greater influence. The resulting variable importance scores identify which moderators drive heterogeneity, and SHAP-style contribution analysis explains each study's position relative to moderator values.

Van Lissa CJ. Journal of Open Source Software 2020;5:2993. SHAP: Lundberg SM, Lee SI. NeurIPS 2017.

Technical Details

Robust pooling engine: DPD with tuning parameter α (0 = MLE, 1 = highly robust). Iterative reweighted least squares converging to the M-estimator. Breakdown point increases with α.

RTMA engine: Models the selection process as left-truncation of the z-score distribution at the affirmative threshold. EM algorithm estimates the true mean and variance of population effects. MAN uses only studies with p > 0.05 or effect in the unexpected direction.

MetaForest engine: Precision-weighted bagged trees with k moderator features. Variable importance via permutation. Out-of-bag R² for model fit. SHAP approximation via marginal contributions.

Privacy: All computations run entirely in-browser. No data leaves your machine.
